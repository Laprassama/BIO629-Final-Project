Quantitative Dissection of the TMB Paradox: The Role of STK11 and KEAP1

Why does high TMB fail in certain patients? We utilized the MSK-CHORD cohort (N=7,471) to investigate how specific genomic resistance drivers neutralize immunotherapy benefits.

About the Project

Tumor Mutational Burden (TMB) is a primary biomarker for identifying candidates for Immune Checkpoint Inhibitors (ICI). However, clinical outcomes are often inconsistent. This project focuses on Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC) to resolve the "TMB Paradox" by integrating secondary genomic drivers.

We hypothesized that STK11 and KEAP1 mutations create a dominant immunosuppressive microenvironment that overrides the neoantigen load signaled by high TMB. Our research objective is to provide a multi-modal "Immuno-Score" to improve survival prediction.

Methods & Statistical Modeling

Our analytical pipeline combines classical biostatistics with machine learning algorithms:

  • Survival Analysis: Kaplan-Meier estimators and log-rank tests were used for univariate comparisons.
  • Interaction Cox Model: A multivariate Cox Proportional Hazards model was constructed with explicit interaction terms (TMB × STK11 and TMB × KEAP1) to quantify biological antagonism.
  • Machine Learning: Random Survival Forest (RSF) with 500 decision trees was employed to rank genomic features by permutation importance.
  • Validation: Time-dependent ROC (tROC) and Brier scores were used to assess 2-year survival predictive accuracy.
